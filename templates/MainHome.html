Overview

CHRONIC KIDNEY DISEASE


Overview

Chronic kidney disease is a general term for heterogeneous disorders affecting kidney structure and function. Signs and symptoms of chronic kidney disease develop over time if kidney damage progresses slowly. Signs and symptoms of kidney disease may include:
  • Nausea
  • Vomiting
  • Loss of appetite
  • Fatigue and weakness
  • Sleep problems
  • Swelling of feet and ankles
  • Muscle twitches and cramps
  • Chest pain, if fluid builds up around the lining of the heart

This list does not include all possible symptoms.
